The Augmented Reality Exploration
Project

August 8th, 2023

The Problem

Exploring cities with the tools available today presents its fair share of challenges.
While modern technology has undoubtedly made exploration more accessible, reliance on digital maps and GPS can lead to an over-reliance on screens, causing us to miss out on the subtle nuances and hidden gems that cities have to offer.

The Solution

Augmented reality has the potential to revolutionize the way we explore cities while staying present in the moment.
By overlaying digital information onto the real-world environment, AR can enhance our understanding and appreciation of the places we visit.

Phase 1
User Research Plan

Background

The background for an AR exploration app involves recognizing the growing popularity and potential of AR technology. AR has opened up exciting possibilities for creating interactive and immersive experiences, enhancing how people explore and interact with the world around them.
Traditional exploration apps and travel guides have limitations in providing a truly engaging and captivating experience.
The idea of developing an AR exploration app emerges to bridge this gap and revolutionize how people discover and learn about different locations, historical sites, landmarks & cultural points of interest.

Goals

Through user research, we aimed to understand the user's preferences, behaviours and pain points regarding their previous experience and knowledge regarding AR.
This research will guide the development of the app, ensuring that it offers;

  • An immersive exploration experience using AR technology.
  • Easy navigation and a user-friendly interface.
  • Offer educational and informative content about various locations.

Methodology

After creating a Proto-Persona, our team brainstormed and came up with 5 Research Questions for our project, from which we were able to devise an interview script and survey questions.
In order to collect Quantitative Data, we conducted a survey online.
Then, in order to collect Qualitative Data, we conducted User Interviews with Six participants.

Phase 2
Definition & Ideation

Problem Statement

Urban explorers and travelers who seek authentic and immersive city experiences encounter challenges in exploring unfamiliar locations that provide personalized and relevant information.
Existing solutions lack the integration of organic discovery with modern technology, leading to less than ideal exploration experiences.
How might we alleviate the struggles of urban explorers and travelers seeking authentic and immersive city experiences, when exploring different locations?

User Insights

During our user research (surveys & interviews), we discovered when exploring a city, users want to find information based on their personal preferences but often are unable to do so as the internet often caters to mainstream audiences by providing generalized lists of activities rather than tailoring suggestions to individual interests.
Therefore, we believe that unless a user does an extensive deep dive into the obscure corners of the internet, they’re unable to find activities that cater to their desires. We might be able to help if we create a way for users to explore their cities in an organic way whilst also being given suggestions based on their interests.

Phase 3
Prototype

Prototype

During this section, we had already studied the competitive landscape.
We worked to define and represent the interactions potential users will have with the product.
We did that by sketching paper wireframes to create rough ideas for our UI and information architecture. Then we converted the paper wireframes to digital wireframes and prototyped them to create our first iteration of the user flow.

User Flow Diagram

Before we began to create wireframes for the application, our team created a user flow diagram which would act as a blueprint for the functionality of the app. This would allow us to create a flow in which the user would be able to use the application.
The user flow allowed us to look at how we would design the onboarding process, signing up, and how we would create the augmented reality overlays related to navigation.

Paper Sketches

During this step, each member of the team created their own paper wireframes, interpreted individually from the user flow diagram we created.

Paper Sketches To Digital Wireframes

We had a clear vision on what we wanted to do regards to designing the UI and functionality of the application. Our focus was on how how the elements and iconography would appear on the screen and how the user would be able to interact with the app.

Phase 4
User Testing & Mockups

Usability Testing Plan

In this section, we came up with a user testing plan from which we could assess the UI and functionality of our prototype.
Based on our testing insights, we would then be able to further iterate upon our digital wireframes & prototype and then create a high-fidelity prototype along with mockups of our final product.

Objective

To evaluate the usability, functionality, and user experience of the AR city exploration app.

  1. Can the users navigate around the app without being confused?
  2. Is the prototype simple and intuitive for the user to use?
  3. Are the users able to scan their environment and locate nearby events without any issues?

Usability Testing Results

Once we had conducted user tests for our prototype, we compiled the data into the sticky notes.
The users had very few and minor issues with the app and most of the issues that were highlighted required only superficial changes to the application UI which we rectified in our next iteration.
